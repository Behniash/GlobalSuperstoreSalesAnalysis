# Global Superstore Sales Analysis

## Overview

This project presents an end-to-end data analysis of the Global Superstore dataset using Python. The objective is to transform raw retail transaction data into actionable business insights through data cleaning, feature engineering, exploratory data analysis (EDA), and business analysis.

The project demonstrates a complete analytics workflow and focuses on understanding sales performance, profitability, customer behavior, product performance, and regional trends.

---

## Business Objectives

- Analyze sales and profit performance.
- Evaluate customer purchasing behavior.
- Identify top-performing and underperforming products.
- Measure the impact of discounts on profitability.
- Analyze regional and market performance.
- Provide data-driven business recommendations.

---

## Dataset

The analysis is based on the Global Superstore transactional dataset covering the period from **2011 to 2014**.

| Metric | Value |
|---------|-------:|
| Total Sales | $12.64M |
| Total Profit | $1.47M |
| Orders | 25,035 |
| Customers | 1,590 |
| Analysis Period | 2011–2014 |

---

## Key Findings

- Sales increased consistently between 2011 and 2014.
- Q4 generated the highest sales and profit.
- Technology was the most profitable product category.
- Furniture generated high sales but relatively low profitability.
- Approximately 24.5% of orders resulted in financial losses.
- Higher discount levels were associated with lower profitability.
- Phones and Copiers were the strongest-performing product subcategories.
- APAC generated the highest sales, while the United States produced the highest profit.
- Pareto analysis showed that approximately 42.5% of customers generated 80% of total sales.

---

## Business Recommendations

- Review the current discount strategy.
- Increase investment in Technology products.
- Re-evaluate Furniture pricing.
- Investigate loss-making countries.
- Prepare inventory before Q4.
- Strengthen customer retention strategies.
